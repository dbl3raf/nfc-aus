South-east Queensland Nocturnal Flight Call Library

This library documents nocturnal flight calls recorded over South-east Queensland. Each species has a page, which contains examples of the call types it is known to give. Unknown calls are multitudinous and we are gradually organising these into groups and have given them interim codes or names to help us remember them as we continue annotating new data. Evidence levels

Because many Australian species have never previously been studied in this context, we are currently unsure about many of the identifications, and have assigned an evidence level reflecting the strength of supporting information. See the accounts for each call type on the species pages.

Confirmed
The call has been directly linked to the species through unequivocal evidence, such as a photographed or visually observed bird producing the sound.

Strongly supported
The identification is very strongly supported by acoustic similarity to curated reference recordings and by ecological context, with no plausible alternative species.

Probable
Evidence suggests the call belongs to the proposed species, but some uncertainty remains, for example due to limited reference recordings or possible confusion species.

Tentative
A working hypothesis based on limited evidence. The call resembles the proposed species but additional data are needed to confirm the identification.

Unresolved
A distinct call type has been recognised but the species producing it is currently unknown.
